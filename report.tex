\section{1. Dataset}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We use two Kaggle CSV files covering Netflix's catalog up to 2025:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{netflix\_movies\_detailed\_up\_to\_2025.csv} — $\sim$16\,000 movies
  \item \textbf{netflix\_tv\_shows\_detailed\_up\_to\_2025.csv} — $\sim$16\,000 TV shows
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Key fields:} \texttt{title}, \texttt{date\_added}, \texttt{release\_year}, \texttt{vote\_average} (IMDb-style rating), \texttt{vote\_count}, \texttt{genres}, \texttt{country}, etc.

% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{2. Visualisations}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Our dashboard contains four interactive D3.js visualisations.

% ─── Chart 1 ────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Chart 1: Content Quality \& Volume (Bubble Chart)}

\textbf{Question:} \textit{How has the volume and quality of Netflix content evolved over time?}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{chart1.png}
    \caption{Evolution of quality (IMDb Rating) vs Volume (Bubble Area) from 2010 to 2025.}
\end{figure}

Each bubble represents a cohort of titles. Bubble \textbf{area} encodes the number of titles. Users toggle between \textbf{Both}, Movies (red), and TV Shows (blue). A green band highlights the "high quality" zone ($\geq$7.0).

% ─── Chart 2 ────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Chart 2: Genre Ecosystem \& Balance (Sunburst / Treemap)}

\textbf{Question:} \textit{How is Netflix's catalog distributed across genres?}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{chart2.png}
    \caption{Hierarchical breakdown of genres showing the Reality TV segment (2.5\% of catalog).}
\end{figure}

A hierarchical visualisation with two modes: \textbf{Sunburst} and \textbf{Treemap}. Clicking a genre segment zooms in to show its Movies/TV Shows breakdown.

% ─── Chart 3 ────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Chart 3: Geographic Diversity \& Market Evolution (Streamgraph)}

\textbf{Question:} \textit{Is Netflix becoming more internationally diverse?}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{chart3.png}
    \caption{Streamgraph of production countries with Simpson's Diversity Index overlay.}
\end{figure}

The \textbf{Simpson's Diversity Index} overlay (dashed amber line) quantifies concentration: values closer to 1.0 indicate a more balanced international production.

% ─── Chart 4 ────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Chart 4: Content Release Heatmap (Month $\times$ Year)}

\textbf{Question:} \textit{When does Netflix add the most content? Are there seasonal patterns?}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{chart4.png}
    \caption{Heatmap showing titles added per month. Significant growth is visible in early 2025.}
\end{figure}

Darker cells indicate higher content volume. Users can filter by content type to see specific acquisition strategies.

\section{4. Challenges \& Limitations}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Challenges}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Multi-valued fields:} Handling titles belonging to multiple countries/genres.
  \item \textbf{Performance:} Rendering 32,000+ rows in the browser with smooth interactions.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Rating bias:} Small sample sizes for certain niche genres might skew IMDb averages.
  \item \textbf{Client-side Only:} Initial load time increases as the CSV dataset grows.
\end{itemize}
